I Hear My School Singing

I hear my school singing, the varied carols I hear.

Those of scholars, each one singing his as it should be hopeful and cheerful.

The students singing theirs as they pull out their pencils or notebooks.

The teachers singing theirs as they make ready for lesson, or leave off for lessons.

The counselors singing what belongs to them in their office, the principal singing on the stage.

The band kids singing as they sit on their chairs, the percussionists singing theirs as they stand.

The bus drivers’ song, the parents on their way in the morning, or at noon or at the evening activities.

The beautiful singing of the chorus, or of the young musicians at the school, or of the custodian cleaning.

Each singing what belongs to him or her and to none else.

The day what belongs to the day — at morning the journey of young teenagers for more knowledge and wisdom.

Singing with open mouths their strong melodious songs.

An Earth Without Humans

What will an earth look like without humans?

Will it be more beautiful

Like a huge garden?

Will it be more ugly

Without any cities and buildings?

Is it more quiet

Without chatter and laughter?

Or will it be filled with sounds

Of the howling wind and singing birds?

Maybe it will remain the same

As a shiny pearl resting in the vast ocean of the universe.

Seem To Be

I seem to be a pig, basking in the sun and relaxing in the mud.

But really I’m a rabbit, running and jumping endlessly.

I seem to be a rock, stiff and rigid.

But really I’m a gust of wind, flowing through the woods and mountains.

I seem to be a winter day, quiet and solemn.

But really I’m a sunny day, passionate and enthusiastic.

I seem to be a piano, calm and graceful.

But really I’m a saxophone, noisy and cheerful.

I seem to be an apple, loving and sweet.

But really I’m a durian, having spikes outside but greatness inside.

I seem to be a GPS, always knowing my directions.

But really I’m a boat in the darkness, never knowing what will happen next.

Arrival

Mom, Dad,

Are we there yet?

I know I already asked this before,

But are we in America yet?


The answer was no,

I had to suffer more.

And I knew the truth

That I couldn’t take this anymore.


This place is like hell,

Filled with weird smells.

I turned up the screen,

Doing my hourly routine.


Movies after movies,

Shows after shows.

I began to lose my temper,

But I couldn’t let it show.


After several hours,

The plane stopped flying.

We arrived in America,

With the hope of a better living.

I Am Hopeful Because I Am Young

I am hopeful because I am young, like a dandelion seed, carried by the wind into the unknown.

I am hopeful because I am young, like a spark of flame, bringing warmth to those around me.

I am hopeful because I am young, like a drop of snowflake, simplistic from afar yet intricate up close.

I am hopeful because I am young, like a flash of lightning, confident that I can outrun anything.

I am hopeful because I am young, like a sprouting seedling, collecting knowledge from the earth around me.

I am hopeful because I am young, like an immovable rock, withstanding the trials of rains and storms.

I am hopeful because I am young, like a small stream, flowing across a thousand mountains into the vast ocean.

But there will come a day when I am not young, will I still be hopeful then?

I do think so, because the memory of my youth will continue to shine like radiating gold, engraved in my memories.

So that I will be young again, and hopeful again.

Fossil

As I gaze into the fossil

First it is a piece of bone

And then it is a coffin buried under the ground

And then it is suffocating in the deep soil

And then it is like the meat between the sandwich

And then it becomes a treasure

And now it is elevating to surface level

And now it is being digged out of the ground

And now I am a archaeologist

Love That Lady

Love that lady, like a bird loves to fly

I said I love that lady, like a bird loves to fly

Love to have a conversation with her, love to call her

‘My beloved mother!’


She talk tenderly

Smiles like an angel

I said she talk tenderly

And smiles like an angel

Smiles when she is in a good mood

When I’m happy, she smiles again


I like to hug her, like to discuss philosophy with her

I said I like to hug her, like to discuss philosophy with her

She is wise and smart

Giving me all the answers I need


She spends a lot of time with me, before the rising moon

I said she spends a lot of time with me, before the rising moon

No matter what happens

She will always be my beloved mother
